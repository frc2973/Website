BEST Competitions

Each year, BEST releases a new theme for the annual competition. On Kickoff Day, the game is revealed and teams begin building the robot. Teams are only allowed to use the materials provided in the kit of parts. This year's game is called MADE2ORDER.

FIRST Competitions

Like BEST, FIRST releases a new theme each year for the annual competition. On Kickoff Day, the game is revealed and teams begin building the robot. Unlike BEST, teams are allowed to use whatever materials they have. This year's game is called CHARGED UP.

BEST Advancement

Seeding Matches

Competition begins with seeding matches, where every team plays 5-8 head-to-head matches against other teams. At the end, teams are ranked based on the total score across their matches. Their lowest scoring match will be dropped and not counted as part of the total. The top 7 or top 15 ranked teams (depending on the total number of teams) will automatically advance to semifinals.

Wildcard Match

Of the teams that did not automatically advance to semifinals, the 4 or top 8 teams (depending on the total number of teams) with the highest Engineering Notebook scores will play 1 more head-to-head match against each other. The highest scoring team will advance to the semifinals.

Semifinals

The teams who made it to this round will play 3 head-to-head matches against the other teams. The teams will be ranked once again based on the total score across all of their matches. Unlike the seeding round, their lowest scoring match will not be dropped. The 4 highest ranked teams will advance to finals.

Finals

In the championship round, the 4 final teams play 3 head-to-head matches against each other. The team with the highest combined score across the 3 matches wins the competition. Afterwards, we have awards.

FIRST Advancement

Qualification Matches

FRC matches are played on an alliance with 2 other teams. During a match, teams race to not only gain match points and win, but also ranking points, which are awarded to the entire alliance. During the qualification round, teams play a series of head-to-head matches in which they are randomly placed on an alliance. At the end, teams are ranked based on the average number of ranking points they have earned across all of their matches.

Alliance Selections

After the qualification matches, the top 8 teams, called alliance captains, have the opportunity to create their own permanent alliance for the playoffs. Going in order from the highest seed to the lowest seed, alliance captains take turns selecting a team to be on their alliance. A selected team may not already be on an alliance and can choose to either accept or decline the invitation. If the selected team declines, the alliance captain selects another team and the process repeats until a team accepts. Once a team has been selected, they cannot be selected again, regardless of whether they accepted or declined the invite. If a team that is set to be an alliance captain joins another alliance, the teams ranked below them move up to fill the empty spot. Once all 8 alliance captains have selected an alliance partner, the selection process repeats for the second alliance partners, this time going from the lowest seed to the highest seed. Once the 8 alliances for playoffs have been established, alliance selections conclude.

Playoff Matches

After alliance selections, playoff matches begin. Alliances compete in a single elimination bracket and advance by winning the best of 3 matches against the opposing alliance. There are no ranking points in the playoffs. At the end, we have awards. The winning alliance captain, winning alliance first pick, and other award winners advance to the championship.
